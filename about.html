I am an economist who studies the economics of digitization and the economics of search and matching markets . I am especially interested in using data on user behavior to inform the design of online marketplaces. I am currently an economics of digitization post-doc at the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER) and a part-time data scientist at Airbnb. I received a Ph.D. in Economics from Stanford University in 2014 and a B.S. in Math and Econ from Duke University in 2008. In my free time I climb, write, watch movies, and walk around discussing philosophy.

Please contact me if you have any data I might find interesting.
