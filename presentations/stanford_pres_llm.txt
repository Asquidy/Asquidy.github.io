- This paper: **industrial organization** of the AI industry
<<<

# Slide(3):

TopTitle: Why focus on the industrial organization of AI?

TopText.8: 
<<<
- Demand Side: 
  - How much more utility does a 'better' model generate? 
  - What characteristics of models do end-users care about? How correlated are these with public benchmarks?
  - How quickly do better models diffuse?
- Supply Side: 
   - How do firms compete with each other? 
   - Is there substantial market power? 
   - What is the role of open source? 
- Combined: What are the private and/or social returns? Anti-trust / regulation? 
<<<

# Slide(4):

TopTitle: This is a descriptive analysis of the industry

TopText.8: 
<<<
Use two data sources: OpenRouter and Microsoft Azure Foundry, with coverage between 2022 and 2025.
Break: 3
Text.8:
**Document:**
- Models, creators, and providers.
- Pricing trends, and prices per unit of intelligence.
- Diffusion, market shares, and concentration. Also, by use case and industry.
- Event studies of market entry.
<<<

# Slide(5):

TopTitle: Main findings

TopText.8: 
<<<
- For similar levels of *measured* intelligence, closed-source models are 7 times more expensive, but are dominant.
- Intelligence-adjusted prices have fallen by approximately 100-fold since the launch of GPT-4.
- New models reach peak market share quickly, within a few weeks on OpenRouter and within 3 - 6 months on Azure. 
- Demand for models is differentiated by brand and use case.
- Adoption of reasoning models is very limited.
- The market for open-source model inference is competitive with free entry.
<<<

TopTitle: LLM Creators 

TopText:

Creators are entities (e.g., OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, Meta) who develop and train LLMs. 
<<< 
- Develop model architecture.
- Assemble data.
- Conduct training runs.
<<<

# Slide(8):

TopTitle: LLM Inference Providers 

TopText.8:

Inference providers are entities who use computing resources to return tokens from a model.
<<< 
- AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud Platform offer inference for closed and open source models.
- OpenAI and Anthropic run their own endpoints.
- Together AI, Cerebras, Groq, and others specialize in inference services, focusing on open-source models.
<<<

# Slide(10):

TopTitle: LLM Aggregators and OpenRouter

Text.8:
LLM aggregators are platforms that sit between developers and model providers. 

They lower switching costs and offer other conveniences.

OpenRouter is the biggest one, serving over 5 trillion tokens per week.

# Slide(12):

TopTitle: Azure Foundry Data

TopText.8: 
- Foundry allows customers to access models hosted by Microsoft through an API.
- Serves OpenAI and xAI models, but not Anthropic or Google models. Also serves open-source models.
- Customer-day level usage + customer characteristics.
